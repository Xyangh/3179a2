These two graphs respectively show the number of universities in the world's top 500 owned by different countries, and provide the average age of these universities. 5 means the school has a history of more than 100 years, 4 means 50-100, 3 means 25-50, 2 means 10-25, and 1 means 0-10.

There's a noticeable positive correlation between the two metrics. Universities that have a higher score in employer reputation tend to also have a higher employment outcomes score. This implies that the reputation of a university among employers may influence or reflect the employment outcomes of its graduates. There are some data points (universities) with high employment outcome scores but moderate employer reputation scores. Conversely, there are also universities with high employer reputation scores but middling employment outcome scores.
