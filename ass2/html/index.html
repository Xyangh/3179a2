QS Rank Data Visualization

Map data visualization

The map shows the number of universities in each country. By dragging the zoom and selecting the map center, you can zoom in to the place you want to observe. You can set the number of universities you want to choose by dragging the 2024 Universities Ranking (top). The figure shows that the number of universities in the United States has always been ahead of the number of universities in other countries. Among the top 100 universities in the world, the United States accounts for 27, followed by 17 universities in the United Kingdom and 9 universities in Australia.

Number of universities by country

This bar chart can be selected by dragging Top_universities and Countries_num. It can be found that among the top 100 universities in the world to the top 1000 universities in the world, the United States always has the largest number of universities, followed by the United Kingdom. China and Australia have always remained in the top four. It shows that the education level of these countries is relatively high, and the number of universities is also considerable.

University Subject Range and University Age

This is about the proportion of the world's top 100 universities in the Subject Range and the proportion of age. Full comprehensive (FC) represents All 5 faculFCty areas + medical school, Comprehensive (CO) represents All 5 faculty areas, Focused (FO) represents 3 or 4 faculty areas, Specialist (SP) represents 2 or fewer faculty areas, where age is 5=100 years old and more, 4=50-99 years old , 3=25-49 years old, 2=10-24 years old and 1=Less than 10 years old. It can be found that 80% of universities are Full Comprehensive universities among the top 100 universities in the world, and 70% of universities have a long history.

Globalization

This chart provides a visual comparison of globalization indicators across different countries. Compares International Faculty, International Students and International Research Network. Compared with other countries, although the United States and China have a large number of universities, their overall internationalization score does not seem to be very high. In contrast, both Australia and the UK seem to have higher internationalization scores. It shows that these countries seem to be more popular with international teachers and international students. This also shows that universities in these countries have a large number of international students. For students who want to study in other countries, these countries with high internationalization scores seem to be a good choice.

Employer Satisfaction and Employment Rate Analysis

It is observed that as employment outcome scores increase, employer reputation scores also tend to be higher. This shows that it seems easier for students from universities recognized by most employers to find jobs. However, there is also some data that although the Employer Reputation scores are high, the Employment Outcomes scores do not seem to be particularly high. These schools are very few.

Analysis of Academic Reputation and Employment

This chart screens the top 50 universities in the world. Each point in this scatter plot represents a university, and its color indicates the country to which it belongs. The size of the dots corresponds to the college's overall score, with larger dots indicating higher scores. The academic reputation of most universities is clustered between 40-100, indicating that top universities generally perform well in academic reputation.

For both employment outcomes and academic reputation, some universities scored higher on academic reputation but lower on employment outcomes, suggesting that a good academic reputation is not always directly related to better employment outcomes. But for employer reputation and academic reputation scores, higher academic reputation seems to have higher employer reputation. This suggests that a good academic reputation seems to be more attractive to employers.

While higher-ranked universities generally score higher on employer reputation, employment outcomes and academic reputation, there are some exceptions, such as France's Ecole Polytechnique, which ranks among the top 50 universities globally. It has the lowest academic reputation but has very high employer reputation scores(99.6) and employment outcomes scores (90.3).
